Inside a list — 3 tiers

Restructured around urgency instead of one undifferentiated list. Now / Could wait / In cart. Cross-list awareness via "→ also on Costco run" or "→ prefer Costco" indicators.

Try it: See how Cholula (out + on Costco) lives in "Now" with a link back to Costco. Butter (low + prefer Costco) lives in "Could wait" — bought anywhere it clears both. Sort within "Could wait" lets you reorder (by category is the default).

The olive oil case

  • Normal state: You're low on olive oil. Goes to Could wait with "→ prefer Costco." You'll get it next Costco run.
  • Out of it today: Dot goes to red on pantry staples. List item auto-promotes to Now. Indicator: "→ also on Costco run." Buy a small bottle locally today, both lists clear.
  • Staple flagged low AND in this week's recipe: Also auto-promotes to Now (reason: "for Tyler's dinner · Sat"). Same routing.

Data model

  • Existing field: grocery_list_items.priority — already has 'needed' | 'nice_to_have'. Now = needed, Could wait = nice_to_have. No migration.
  • Cross-list: same ingredient on two lists = query-time join. When one list's item is checked off, the other list's copy auto-dismisses (same ingredient_id + recent timestamp).
  • Promotion logic: "why is this in Now?" tracked via priority_reason text — "staple out" / "for X recipe" / "manual." User can drag down to Could wait explicitly.

Cook post depletion — three options

All three respect your "passive, not forced" direction. Differences are where depletion happens in the flow and how much agency the user has at each step.

Option A · Banner after post (recommended)

  • Cook post flow is unchanged. Post as you would today.
  • After success, a banner appears: "✓ Posted · We updated your pantry · 4 items · review / undo"
  • Silent action by default. Two escape hatches (review / undo).
  • Assumes the common case ("yes, deplete what the recipe called for") and trusts the user to undo if wrong.

Option B · Inline toggle at post

  • One small row at the bottom of compose: "Update pantry · 4 items" with toggle.
  • User sees what's being affected without having to tap through a sheet.
  • Toggle defaults on. Tap the row to expand what will be depleted.
  • More visible than A, but adds a UI element to every post.

Option C · Ask later (post-first)

  • Cook post flow has no depletion UI at all. Post and move on.
  • Next time user opens Pantry, a banner: "You cooked X yesterday · Review / Skip"
  • Most passive. But creates a time gap where pantry state is out of date.
  • If user never opens pantry after cooking, depletion never happens.
Try it: Toggle between A / B / C with the sub-toggle at the top of the phone mockup. Notice how A is the fastest flow (cook → post → done), B is most explicit, C is most deferrable.

My take

A wins for most users — fast, trust-based, reversible. C is great for power users or passive cookers (Mary logs a cook she forgot about, doesn't want to think about pantry). B is the safe middle. If you pick one for v1 I'd do A; if you want to offer choice, Pantry settings could let users switch the default.

Browse recipes — simpler

Two directions to react to. Both drop the long section-scroll from v2.

Option 1 · Question-led tiles

  • Opens with your question: "What are you looking for?"
  • 2×3 grid of tiles, each answering one browsing motivation with a count.
  • Tap a tile → drill into that filtered list.
  • Search box tucked at the bottom for direct-search users.
  • Pro: radically simpler entry. Forces the "why am I browsing?" decision first.
  • Con: users who want to search or scroll immediately have to tap through.

Option 3 · Search-first with chips

  • Big search bar at the top.
  • Chips beneath as quick filters (Quick / Classics / Friends / New / Expiring / All).
  • One hero section below — "Jump back in" — everything else drives from search or chip.
  • Pro: respects power users, structured shortcuts for browsers.
  • Con: empty feeling on open, less discoverable than tiles.
Try it: Toggle between the two at the top of the phone mockup. Think about which mode your Tuesday-evening self would actually use.

My take

I'd ship Option 1 for F&F. The "What are you looking for?" frame makes the app feel like it's asking — which matches how real browsing actually starts. Option 3 is more of a power user view; add it as a setting toggle or an alternate mode once F&F feedback tells us whether tiles feel right.

Freezer cleanout — new surface

Addresses Mary's "we need to do a deep freezer inventory" comment. Surfaces forgotten items proactively, offers three actions per item, enables async planning so nothing gets dropped.

Try it:
• Tap 🍳 Find recipes on the chicken — shows what you can cook with it + pantry.
• Tap ❄️ Thaw & plan — item moves to the Thawing tray at the bottom with a planned date.
• The Thawing tray also appears on the Pantry tab so Mary sees it tomorrow morning without navigating here.

The async-planning pattern

This is the core idea — small commitment now, the app remembers, surfaces later. "I'm going to thaw this chicken" is a 2-second action, and tomorrow the chicken appears on the Pantry's Thawing tray with a soft reminder. Saturday rolls around, if no meal has been planned, a nudge: "Thaw chicken · no meal planned yet · pick one?"

Cross-ingredient planning

  • Find recipes on a freezer item → goes to multi-ingredient recipe search pre-filtered by that ingredient + your pantry state.
  • Multi-select → "I'm pulling out the chicken AND some peas from freezer" → "what can I make with both + pantry?"
  • At the store — Tom picks up cilantro at the market, the app knows about the thawed chicken, presents "cilantro + chicken" recipes.

Items auto-qualify for freezer cleanout

Any pantry item where storage_location='freezer' AND last_touched_at < now - 60 days. "Touched" = state changed, quantity adjusted, or tagged in a cook post. Low-bar heuristic that catches the long tail without false positives.

Out of scope (deferred)

  • Smart thaw-time calculation (4 lb chicken = 24 hours — useful but not v1)
  • Auto-scheduling onto meal calendar (manual date pick for v1, auto in Phase 9)
  • Notifications/reminders at the app level (uses in-app banners for v1)
  • AI visual freezer recognition (post-launch moonshot)

  5. Grocery 3-tier — Now / Could wait / In cart. Tier names feel right? Cross-list indicator ("→ also on Costco run") clear?
  6. Freezer cleanout threshold — 60+ days untouched. Too lax, too aggressive?
